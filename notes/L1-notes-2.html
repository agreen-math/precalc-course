    Chapter Functions

    Lesson Objectives.

    • Find domain &range of functions.
    • Determine symmetry of functions.
    • Graph functions using transformations.
    • Write equations for transformed functions.
    • Find compositions of functions.
    • Find inverses of functions.
    • Use graphs to solve inequalities.
    • Solve inequalities using the test-point method.

    Section Function Vocabulary

    Definition 1. domain.
    The domain of a function \(f\) is the largest set of real numbers for which the value \(f(x)\) is a real number. Things to look out for
    • Division by zero
    • Even roots of negative numbers
    NOTE: For word problems/verbal descriptions, the domain depends on the real-world context of the model.
    Example 2.
    Finding the domain of a function from an equation
    \(f(x)=3x+2\) \(g(x)=\dfrac{3x+2}{x+1}\) \(h(x)=\sqrt{x-3}\)
    Definition 3. zero.
    If \(f\) is a function and \(r\) is a real number for which \(f(r)=0\), then \(r\) is a zero of \(f\text{.}\) In other words, \(r\) is an x-intercept of the graph of \(f\text{.}\)
    Definition 4. maximum.
    The maximum of a function \(f\) is the largest value in the range of \(f\text{.}\) On the graph of \(f\text{,}\) the maximum is the y-coordinate of a point at the top of a peak.
    Definition 5. minimum.
    The minimum of a function \(f\) is the smallest value in the range of \(f\text{.}\) On the graph of \(f\text{,}\) the minimum is the y-coordinate of a point at the bottom of a valley.
    Definition 6. even.
    A function \(f\) is even if and only if it is symmetric with respect to the \(y\)-axis. \(f(x)=f(-x)\) for every \(x\)-value in the domain of \(f\)
    Definition 7. odd.
    A function \(f\) is odd if and only if it is symmetric with respect to the origin. \(f(-x)=-f(x)\) for every \(x\)-value in the domain of \(f\)
    Example 8.
    (Determining Symmetry from an Equation) \(f(x)=x^{4}+3x^{2}-x\)

    Section Transformations of Functions

    Definition 9. transformation.
    Transformations are rule-based changes to the graph of a function. Each transformation can occur in one of two directions: vertical or horizontal. There are three types of transformations:
    • Translation- This represents a change in location.
    • Dilation- This represents a change in size.
    • Reflection- This represents a change in orientation.
    Given a function \(f(x)\text{,}\) a transformation of \(f(x)\) is a function of the form
    \arraystretch2
    \(\mathbf{a}\text{:}\) vertical dilation and/or reflection negative: reflect over \(x\)-axis
    \(\mathbf{|a|}>1\) taller    \(\mathbf{|a|}<1\) shorter
    \(\mathbf{b}\text{:}\) horizontal dilation and/or reflection negative: reflect over \(y\)-axis
    \(\mathbf{|b|}>1\) narrower    \(\mathbf{|b|}<1\) wider
    \(\mathbf{c}\text{:}\) horizontal translation \(\mathbf{c}\) positive (subtraction from \(x\)): right
    \(\mathbf{c}\) negative (addition to x): left
    \(\mathbf{d}\text{:}\) vertical translation \(\mathbf{d}\) positive: up
    \(\mathbf{d}\) negative: down
    Note: Follow the order of operations to perform multiple transformations in sequence: \(\mathbf{c}\text{,}\) then \(\mathbf{b}\text{,}\) then \(\mathbf{a}\text{,}\) then \(\mathbf{d}\text{.}\)
    Example 10.
    (Transforming Points)Describe the sequence of transformations of the graph of \(f(x)=x^{2}\) to obtain the graph of \(g(x)=-3(x+2)^{2} -1\text{,}\) then sketch \(g(x)\text{.}\)
    Example 11.
    (Transforming Features)Describe the sequence of transformations of the graph of \(f(x)=|x|\) to obtain the graph of \(g(x)=-3|x+2|-1\text{,}\) then sketch \(g(x)\text{.}\)

    Section Combining and Undoing Functions

    Definition 12. Function Operations &Compositions.
    Given two functions \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\)
    \arraystretch3
    sum “f plus g” \((f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x)\) 34cmprovided \(x\) is common to the domains of both \(f\) and \(g\)
    difference “f minus g” \((f-g)(x)=f(x)-g(x)\)
    product “f times g” \((f\cdot g)(x)=f(x)\cdot g(x)\)
    quotient “f divided by g” \(\left(\dfrac{f}{g}\right)(x)=\dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) provided \(g(x)\neq0\)
    “f composed with g” 34cmprovided \(x\) is in the domain of \(g\) and \(g(x)\) is in the domain of \(f\)
    composition “f after g” \((f\circ g)(x)=f(g(x))\)
    “f of g”
    Definition 13. Inverse Function.
    Let \(f\) and \(g\) be two functions such that
    • \((f\circ g)(x)=f(g(x))=x\) for every \(x\) in the domain of \(g\)
    and
    • \((g\circ f)(x)=g(f(x))=x\) for every \(x\) in the domain of \(f\)
    Then the function \(g\) is called the inverse function of \(\mathbf{f}\) and is denoted by \(f^{-1}\text{.}\)

    Subsection Finding the Inverse of a Function

    • Change \(f(x)\) to \(y\) if needed.
    • Swap \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\)
    • Solve for \(y\text{.}\)
    • Change \(y\) to \(f^{-1}(x)\) if needed.
    • Swap individual coordinates &connect the dots: \((a,b)\) on the original becomes \((b,a)\) on the inverse.
    • Alternatively, reflect the entire graph over the line \(y=x\text{.}\)
    Example 14.
    (Finding the inverse of a function from an equation.) \(f(x)=\dfrac{x+1}{x-2}\)
