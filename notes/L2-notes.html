    Section Polynomials

    Lesson Objectives.

    • Determine roots of polynomial functions, including multiplicity.
    • Determine symmetry of polynomial functions.
    • Determine end behavior of polynomial functions.
    • Write the equation of a polynomial function given the characteristics or a graph of the polynomial function.
    • Solve polynomial equations and inequalities.
    • Sketch the graph of a polynomial function.
    • Solve applications involving polynomial functions or inequalities.

    Subsection Polynomial Functions

    Definition 16. Polynomial Function.

    A polynomial function is a function of the form
    \begin{equation*} f(x)=a_{n}x^{n}+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots+a_{1}x+a_{0} \end{equation*}
    where
    • \(a_{n}\) is a real number called the leading coefficient
    • \(n\ge 0\) is a whole number called the degree
    • The domain of a polynomial function is the set of all real numbers.
    • The graph of a polynomial function is both smooth (no sharp corners or cusps) and continuous (no gaps or holes).

    Subsection The Leading Coefficient Test (End Behavior)

    • Odd degree polynomial functions
      • Ends go toward opposite infinities
    • Even degree polynomial functions
      • Ends go toward the same infinity
    • Positive leading coefficient
      • the right end goes toward positive infinity
    • Negative leading coefficient
      • the right end goes toward negative infinity

    Subsection Turning Points

    If \(f\) is a polynomial function of degree \(n\text{,}\) then the graph of \(f\) has at most \(n-1\) turning points. (This also works in reverse: \(n-1\) turning points means the degree is at least \(n\))

    Subsection Real Zeros and Multiplicity

    If \((x-r)^{m}\) is a factor of a polynomial function \(f\) (where \(m\) is the highest exponent possible), then \(r\) is called a real zero of multiplicity \(m\) of \(f\).
    • If \(r\) has odd multiplicity, the graph will cross the \(x\)-axis at \(x=r\text{.}\)
      • The sign of \(f(x)\) changes from one side to the other.
    • If \(r\) has even multiplicity, the graph will bounce the \(x\)-axis at \(x=r\text{.}\)
      • The sign of \(f(x)\) does not change from one side to the other.
    NOTE: Vocabulary The following statements are equivalent for polynomial functions:
    • \(r\) is a real number for which \(f(r)=0\)
    • \(r\) is a real zero of the polynomial function \(f\)
    • \(r\) is a root of the polynomial function \(f\)
    • \(r\) is an \(x\)-intercept of the graph of \(f\)
    • \((x-r)\) is a factor of the graph of \(f\)
    • \(r\) is a real solution of the equation \(f(x)=0\)

    Subsection SUMMARY: Steps for Graphing a Polynomial Function

    1. Check for symmetry. (optional)
    2. Determine the real zeros of the function and their multiplicities. Use this information to determine whether the graph crosses or bounces on the \(x\)-axis at each \(x\)-intercept.
    3. Determine the end behavior as \(x\rightarrow\infty\) and as \(x\rightarrow-\infty\)
    4. Find the \(y\)-intercept of the graph of the function by evaluating \(f(0)\text{.}\)
    5. Find additional points between intercepts as needed.
    6. Draw a smooth curve through the points to sketch the graph.
